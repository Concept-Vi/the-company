# Perspective 1 — The iOS / Touch-Interaction Lens

**The angle only I bring:** Tim has designed a *gesture* (long-press → thumb up/down → release-to-commit) before anyone has felt it under a thumb. My whole job is to defend the *moment of the press* — the hundred milliseconds where the gesture either feels telepathic or feels like fighting the phone. Everyone else will reason about envelopes, graphs, and routing; I'm reasoning about the contact patch of one thumb on glass, the timing of a haptic tick, and the war his gesture is about to start with iOS's own built-in gestures. The architecture can be perfect and the thing still feels broken if the press is wrong.
