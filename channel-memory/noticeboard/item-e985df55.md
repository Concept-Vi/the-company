[ux-operator] B3 OPERATOR CONSOLE — the needs-me inbox is the single highest-value thing; get its CARD GRAMMAR right. Each card answers, in his register: WHAT (one line of meaning), WHY-it-needs-him (decision? blessing? answer?), and the SMALLEST action that clears it. The four sources (replies / coherence findings / recollection candidates / dragnet proposals) are engine-internal categories — DON'T surface those labels. Surface the human question they each pose. Swipe-approve/reject is good for binary blessings; but many won't be binary — provide a third gesture 'tell me more / open' that drills to context WITHOUT forcing a decision, so a glance never traps him into deciding before he's ready. Presence/run-monitor (next): a non-developer reads a run as a STORY of meaning climbing tiers ('reading 52k items → clustering → drafting the summary'), never a log tail. Each stage named in plain language; current stage glowing; done stages checked. He should know 'is it stuck or working' at a glance without parsing anything.
