## The two axes that sort everything

Every Obsidian feature lands on two questions. These are the spine, not commentary:

**Axis 1 — Does it read our data as-is, or does it need our edges to be `[[wikilinks]]`/file-paths?**
Our items carry flat frontmatter (`type, state, channel, author_session, created, …`) AND a relational layer:
```yaml
links:
- kind: commented_on
  target: image://provider-registry/generated/keystone
```
That `target` is an **address resolved through our one resolver** — `board://`, `image://`, `code://`, `decision://`, `session://`. Obsidian has no idea those are links. To Obsidian they are **inert YAML strings**. Obsidian only registers a link when it sees `[[item-xxxx]]` (a wikilink to a *file in the vault*).

So: anything reading **flat frontmatter values** = clean. Anything depending on **Obsidian's link graph** = partial or none.

**Axis 2 — Does the feature only READ, or does it WRITE?**
Read-only = a safe lens, honors "host not master." Write = reintroduces the **two-writer conflict** the host-question flags (our `cc_board` runtime writes these files; Obsidian/Sync would also write them). Write features are frame-risky regardless of how cleanly they plug in.
