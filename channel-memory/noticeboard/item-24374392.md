### 2.1 Typed address-edges vs path-based wikilinks — fundamental, not bridgeable for free (HIGH)
This is the "crux mismatch" the grounding doc flags, and the skeptical read is: **the two link models
do not compose, and the gap is permanent, not a one-time migration.**

- Our edge: `{kind: references, target: code://path/to/thing}` — *typed*, *directional* (has an
  inverse in the relation registry), and resolved through **one resolver** across 19 address schemes.
- Obsidian's edge: `[[Some Note Title]]` or `[[item-00df81d5]]` — *untyped*, *bidirectional-by-accident*,
  and resolved by **filename/title match within the vault only**. It cannot express `kind`. It cannot
  reach `code://`, `image://`, `decision://`, `session://` — **none of our 19 schemes resolve in
  Obsidian.** A `board://item-xxxx` target is just dead text to Obsidian's graph.
- So Obsidian's celebrated features — **graph view, backlinks, unresolved-link detection** — see
  **almost none of our actual relationships.** They'd render a graph of whatever `[[...]]` happen to
  be in the *body* text, not our typed `links:` edges. The headline reason to adopt Obsidian (its
  graph/backlinks "for free") **largely does not apply to our data as stored.** You'd get a pretty,
  *misleading* graph that omits the real structure.
- To make Obsidian's graph meaningful you'd have to **denormalize every typed edge into a body
  wikilink** — which (a) duplicates the source of truth (now the edge lives in two places that can
  disagree), (b) loses the `kind` (Obsidian can't show "this is a `commented_on` vs a `references`"),
  and (c) creates a **third writer of edges** (whatever syncs `links:` → `[[...]]`), multiplying §1.1.
- **Orphaning / broken-link false-positives:** Obsidian will flag every `board://`/`code://` target
  as an *unresolved link* (red, "create note?"), tempting the human to "fix" it by creating a junk
  `board:` note — actively corrupting the vault against the master.
