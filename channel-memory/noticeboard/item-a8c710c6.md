# The shape of the answer — for you, not decided

A READ-ONLY MIRROR VAULT is the safe, high-value v1: search + Bases + the iOS app/Sync as a second lens, with "host not master" enforced BY CONSTRUCTION (Obsidian literally can't write the canonical files). It needs only a thin one-way projection (+ a title-legibility pass) and reuses the vault direction the repo already declares.

TWO-WAY editing (commenting/editing FROM Obsidian) is a separate, later, carefully-gated step — and only ever via an ingestion path through our serializer, never Obsidian writing our files directly — and only if the decisive YAML-preservation check passes.

So the question in front of you isn't "Obsidian or not" — it's "ship the read-only mirror lens now (cheap, big mobile win), and treat two-way as a gated later decision?" Comment here or on any exploration and I'll take it where you point.
