# Perspective 7 — The Professional Annotator

*My angle:* I mark up documents all day — manuscripts, contracts, papers. I don't "leave feedback," I **redline**. My craft has 400 years of muscle-memory baked into a few hard conventions, and almost none of them are in this design yet. The single biggest thing I see: you've designed how a mark gets *made and sent*, but a professional annotation tool lives or dies on what happens *after* — the mark's **lifecycle**, its **disambiguation**, and the **pass** it belongs to. That's where toy markup tools fall apart and where I can tell you exactly what a real annotator will reach for and not find.
