## Tier 4 — Largely a non-issue (don't waste worry here)

- **"Obsidian can't open the files at all."** Non-issue — they're plain `.md` with YAML frontmatter;
  Obsidian opens them fine. The problem is never *reading*, it's *writing* and *interpreting edges*.
- **Vector/embedding search "needed from Obsidian."** Non-issue — the Company already has its own
  embedding/recall substrate; Obsidian search is redundant, not a gap. (And per Tim's memory, the
  Company corpus memory is explicitly preferred over external plugins.)
- **Canvas / graph view as a *read-only inspection toy* for Tim on desktop.** Genuinely low-risk *if*
  strictly read-only and never written back — a disposable lens. The risk is entirely on the
  write/sync side, not the view side. If Obsidian could be guaranteed **read-only**, most of Tier 1–2
  evaporates. (But "guaranteed read-only on a mobile sync client" is itself hard to enforce — Obsidian
  has no robust read-only-vault mode; the moment Tim taps a property it writes.)
