# The real clashes — where they genuinely disagree (these are YOUR decisions)

A. HOW MUCH TO BUILD NOW. The MINIMALIST says the working loop is already ~80%; ship batch + reply-inbox this week and DEFER the tree, UI-mode, the gesture, and access-gating. The GRAPH-ARCHITECT and ANNOTATION-TOOLS lenses say the tree and the envelope are load-bearing — design them right NOW or you inherit every famous tool's scars. This is the central tension: lean-loop-closer vs foundation-first.

B. "ONE MECHANISM" — IS IT? Your brief (and my breakdown) said both modes and the whole flow are one mechanism. The GRAPH-ARCHITECT pushes back: a board-comment (an edge) and a composed-mark (state from a thread) are TWO distinct runtimes; the envelope should be a board ITEM with a DERIVED mark as its read-projection — not literally one machinery. A correction to the unifying claim worth weighing.

C. TOUCH-FIRST OR VOICE-FIRST? Everyone assumed touch is primary — except the VOICE lens, which INVERTS the whole thing: for someone who prefers speaking, voice IS the comment and the thumb only selects targets and repairs dictation. Given you talk your messages, this reframes a lot. Big fork.

D. THE GESTURE, THREE WAYS: cut it (minimalist) · keep the idea but rewire it to tap-default/long-press-escalate with haptic detents (iOS) · demote it to a fallback under voice (voice).
