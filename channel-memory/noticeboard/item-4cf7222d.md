# FLOW — the steps

**0 · Grounding.** Every agent first loads the pack: the **original project context + project intros**, your vision, your laws + vocabulary, the brief, the prior research/perspectives. Nothing is touched before this frame is loaded.

**1 · Reconnaissance (the pre-step).** Extractors read **every comment WITH its anchored content** (distributed — not one AI). The Cartographer composes them into the **real map**: the actual clusters, the intent-types, the cross-links, and **what each cluster demands as an outcome**. This map **configures the run** — how many room-teams, which lenses, what each targets. Rooms are **discovered, not pre-templated**. The lead works from the map, never raw-comments-solo. *(This is where "you can't know the outcomes until the comments are read" is answered — it's the pre-step.)*

**2 · Design (per discovered room).** A proposer drafts the room's design (the cluster's intent + research + current design + the real code); critics attack it (diverge); the proposer revises in place. Rounds until the room settles or a genuine fork is named. Discussion happens on the room's working channel.

**3 · Process-Logic audit.** An auditor checks the **whole run's logic**: coverage (every comment accounted for + routed right), proposals genuinely address their comments, no cross-room contradictions, the reasoning chain holds end-to-end.

**4 · Synthesise.** The lead composes across rooms, **preserving tensions (never flattening)**, decides the technical calls, surfaces only the few genuine taste/direction forks (pre-defaulted).

**5 · Land.** Each comment's outcome **re-surfaced as a reply at its anchor**; the master design + map assembled. Nothing declared final.
