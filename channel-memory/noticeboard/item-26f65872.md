## 6. Smaller craft-conventions worth stealing (rapid-fire, grounded)

- **"Global" / house-style marks.** A reviewer often writes one mark meaning *everywhere*: "use Australian spelling throughout," "we always capitalize Company." That's a mark scoped to the *whole document* (or project) but *about a recurring pattern*, not one span. Tim's "comment on the whole document" button (MSG2) is the seed of this. The verb taxonomy + the existing **scope ladder** (A3) give it a home: a global-scoped Change mark = "apply this rule everywhere." The member treats it as a sweep, not a point-edit.

- **Stet.** The most professional mark of all: "I considered your change and I'm rejecting it — keep the original." When a *member* proposes a change and Tim doesn't want it, he needs one tap that means "no, leave it" *and explains nothing*. Distinct from ignoring. It closes the thread as **rejected-by-author**, which the member must respect (not re-propose). Lawyers live on this.

- **Severity / priority is a real axis, not decoration.** Proofreaders distinguish a *must-fix* (factual error) from a *preference* (I'd prefer "whilst"). The brief mentions priority once (BD-D ADDITIONS). Make it a property of the mark Tim can flip — because his members will batch-triage by it. Two levels is enough on a phone: **must** vs **nice-to-have**. Don't over-engineer; editors use exactly two.

- **The query needs a structured ANSWER slot, not just a reply.** When a member poses Tim a choice (BD-F "choices/selections offered"), that's the inverse of an editorial query — *the member querying the author*. Professional tools give these a constrained answer widget (pick A/B/C, yes/no) so the answer is unambiguous and machine-actionable. A free-text reply to "should this be 0.5 or 0.6?" can come back as "yeah the second one" — ambiguous again. The radial-menu's "needs your eyes" lane (BD-G) should render these as **tappable decisions**, not chat.

- **Don't show me the content I selected — show me my MARK.** MSG2 nails this ("it's already on screen highlighted"). The professional version: the composer should show the **verb + my draft**, anchored visually to the highlighted span by a connecting line/color, *not* re-quote the text. The connection between mark and target is *spatial*, not textual. This is exactly his instinct, stated as craft principle.

- **A mark can attach to a SPAN that crosses block boundaries.** "This argument, from the second paragraph through the bullet list, contradicts the heading." Real reviewers mark *ranges* that don't respect the tree. The hierarchy gesture only selects *nodes/subtrees*. Reserve one of his empty left/right slots (BD-B) for **"extend selection to here"** — start a span, scroll, end it — so a mark can cover an arbitrary range, not only a clean subtree. Editors need this constantly; the tree is a convenience, not a cage.
