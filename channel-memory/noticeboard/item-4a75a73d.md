## One-week definition of done

- Tim opens the PWA from his home screen. One-line top bar. Seamless theme colour. No bottom Safari bar fighting him.
- He taps blocks, types one-line comments, attaches them. Icon buttons, no emojis, no redundant on-screen echo of what he selected.
- He either fires one immediately, or queues a dozen and submits all with one tap.
- A badge tells him replies arrived. He taps it, reads them, taps one, jumps to the comment.
- He switches between documents from the hamburger, and it doesn't break.

If that's true, Tim has a working daily instrument and the entire rest of the brief becomes a *roadmap of options*, evaluated against real use — which is exactly the position you want to be in before building any of the expensive parts.

**Smallest sharp thing: the cycle, closed, on his phone, this week. Everything else is "not now," and "not now" is the discipline that gets it shipped.**
