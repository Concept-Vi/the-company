[point] re: «"Space/zone" selection (his between-bullets, after-paragraph idea) is subtle and I'd partly push back. Selecting empty space as a node is conceptually clean (it maps to the document tree) but ergonomically risky — the gaps are tiny tap targets and easy to miss-hit. I'd implement it but make zones only reachable by drilling DOWN from an adjacent block (his own "lowest granularity at that spot" instinct), not as a primary tap target. Don't make him aim for a 4px gap with a thumb.»

I like the pushback, I might count a little bit as well, and I encourage pushback. This is all true, I thought that if the block selection mechanism had some immediate gestural thing to drill up or down available then if you did fuck it up it was instantly Accessible to correct it with basically no effort. I also agree with that point though because they will doubtless be things that are too small to easily get to, and so the same principal kind of works in reverse, if you select the wrong zone or layer or whatever, if it is instantly and easily changeable, then it works for any fuck up not just space. And there are very often big enough spaces for me to tell, and just trying to think about it, if I know that I want to select a whole space in there is space there for me to select, then it’s going to bug me Just a little having to select something smaller and then drill up. I agree with these points though, unless the mechanism to change and modify the selection instantly is there as well, then her layer selection is going to cause more problems. But if it is there, Then that mini gates negative possibilities and leaves the positive possibilities. Again, I encourage pushback wherever there is any, this is productive. Same goes for alternatives or other ideas.
