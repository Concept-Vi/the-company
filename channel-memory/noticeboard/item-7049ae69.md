### 2. Bases (`.base`) — CLEAN over frontmatter; the strongest *structured* win for Tim.
Bases is **native** (no plugin), **mobile**, and **already present in the vault** (`Obsidian Builder/Dashboard.base`, `Spaces/Mobile Parity/02 Status Board.base`). It builds table/card/list views by **filtering, sorting, grouping on frontmatter**. Our frontmatter is consistent and rich — `type`, `state`, `channel`, `author_session`, `created` — which is exactly Bases' raw material.

For a non-dev on a phone this is the killer view: *"show me every `note` in channel `provider-registry`, newest first"* / *"all items in `state: posted` grouped by channel"* — as a tappable card list, built once, no code. It supersedes Dataview for Tim (see below).

**The honest limit (verified against Obsidian's Bases syntax + forum):** Bases' `link()` and `file.links` operate on Obsidian's **registered outgoing links** — i.e. `[[wikilinks]]` to vault files. They **cannot resolve our `board://`/`image://`/`code://` address targets**, because those aren't vault files and aren't wikilinks. So Bases gives Tim a beautiful *filtered list of items*, but it **cannot walk our typed edges** (`commented_on`, `reply_to`, `part_of`). It sees the items; it can't see the relationships between them. Read-only by nature → Axis 2 green. Axis 1 green for fields, **red for edges.**
