## 5. Where I stay in my lane (prerequisites I name but don't design)

These are real and the brief is right to flag them, but they're other perspectives' lanes — I only note the *data-model consequence*:

- **Membership gates delivery, not access** (verified-by-brief). Data-model consequence: "member X can read/act on this envelope" wants to be **an edge from the envelope-node to the member-node** (`can_read`/`assigned_to` in `board_edges/`), not a separate permission table. Access-as-edges folds into the same graph. *Designing the access model is not my lane.*
- **Three channel systems.** Data-model consequence: the envelope's `channel` field is currently an opaque name that any of the three could claim. Until convergence, **declare which channel-registry is authoritative for routing** so the `channel` field has one referent. *The convergence itself is not my lane.*
- **iOS gesture ergonomics / overlay** — entirely out of my lane; my only contribution is that the gesture must be a *pure cursor over the `part_of` tree* so it carries zero document logic.
