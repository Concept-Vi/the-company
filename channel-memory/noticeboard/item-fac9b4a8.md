[paragraph] re: «And the self-resolution principle you remembered is real and disciplined — richer than I knew. A session finds its own identity through an injected self-marker, never by typing an ID, and the system fails loud if it can't be sure which session is "self" (it refuses to guess, because mis-identifying self would corrupt memory). That's not just convenience — it's a safety property: you can only ever act as yourself, so you can't accidentally (or maliciously) act as another. Your instinct to generalize "self" to "here" (this channel) and "this project" is exactly the relative-root idea, and the foundation for it is already laid (the system already anchors things to a stable root rather than wherever-you-happen-to-be).»

Yeah I think there is more to this Concept. And I think this comes from every ai or Agent actor being registered, just like the members and stuff. That kind of automatic resolution, that applies to the concept of ‘self’ at the individual level, probably at the instance level, at the channel level or the project, or other things, anything that the calling actor belongs to, which again would probably be declared in wherever it is registered. I think there is a lot more possible with this, it doesn’t necessarily only have to be things that an agent users for self resolution stuff, it could be used for things that are outside of an agent intention, I’m sure plenty of things, that kind of resolution. And this is good.
