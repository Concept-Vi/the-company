## The shape of "host not master," read off the axes

The clean reading of the spread: **adopt Obsidian as a read-only lens over our frontmatter, never as the keeper of our edges.**

- The features that honor "host not master" are exactly the **read-only, frontmatter-reading** ones (search, Bases, the iOS reading surface). They give Tim mobile reach and instant structured views **for free** without touching the canonical fabric.
- The features that fight the frame are the **edge-dependent** ones (graph) and the **write** ones (Properties editing, Sync write-back) — because our edges are typed addresses Obsidian can't model, and writes reopen the two-writer conflict.
- **Our typed graph stays ours.** Obsidian's graph view is the wrong lens for it; the value Tim wants there comes from finishing the system's own declared-unbuilt walkable graph (design-map A2/A6), not from a lossy wikilink shadow.

**Single most important caveat to carry forward:** the highest-value item (mobile Obsidian) is gated on the WSL-vs-Windows-sync infra fact, and *all* Obsidian surfaces are gated on the title-legibility fact. Neither is a toggle. Everything else (search, Bases) is genuinely near-free and read-only — those are the clean wins to reach for first.
