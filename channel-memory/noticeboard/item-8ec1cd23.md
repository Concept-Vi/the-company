# Perspective 2 — What mature annotation & review tools already learned (and where the famous ones got it wrong)

**My angle:** I read this brief as a person who has watched Google Docs, Figma, Hypothesis, GitHub, Notion, Medium, and PDF reviewers all build the *same* "comment-on-a-thing" machine — and all hit the *same* small set of brutal failure modes. This system's "typed observation on an addressed node" is architecturally cleaner than any of them. But cleanliness is not safety: the places these tools bleed are precisely the places this brief is thinnest — **anchor survival when the target changes**, **what the agent sees vs. what Tim saw**, and **the gesture/scroll war on a phone**. I'm going to spend my words on those, with named mechanisms and named scars, tied back to the envelope, the blocks, and the two modes.
