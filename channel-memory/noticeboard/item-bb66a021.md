## 8. Routing to people who act — the lesson tools learned late: a comment without an assignee dies

The brief routes envelopes to a supervisor who routes to members. The category's hard lesson (Linear, GitHub, Asana, Notion all converged here): **an unassigned comment is a dropped comment.** Threads with no owner rot. Concrete patterns to bring in:

- **Explicit per-envelope assignee + status lifecycle**, visible to Tim: `received → triaged → working → replied → applied/closed`. This is the breakdown's BD-D/BD-F status idea; I'm naming the proven *states*. Linear's discipline is that every comment-that-implies-work becomes a trackable item with one owner and one status; that's why nothing silently dies.
- **The reply must re-surface at the anchor**, not only in a separate inbox. Medium's biggest engagement failure was burying responses away from the highlight. Tim's overlay (the V/radial) is the inbox; but a reply should *also* show a badge **on the block/element it concerns**, so Tim sees "this one got answered" in context. Two surfaces, one truth.
- **Structured questions back to Tim.** When a member needs a decision, it should return a **typed option-set** (the brief's "choices/selections offered"), rendered as tap-targets in the overlay — not a free-text question Tim has to parse and answer in prose. This is the GitHub "review requested / suggested change → accept" loop generalized: the member proposes structured, Tim taps, the answer folds back as an observation.
