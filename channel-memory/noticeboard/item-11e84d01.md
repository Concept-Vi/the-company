# The thing you need to know — there are actually *three* different things called "channels"

This is a real "what's connected to what" fact, and it matters for "dragnets attached to channels," so I want to surface it plainly rather than let it stay hidden:

1. **The live-messaging transport** — the one you've effectively been using through me. Members are a **flat list of handles**, no posture. Its job is *pushing a message into a running session.* Pure delivery.
2. **The rich work-group system** — the one with awake/listening, coordinator, conducted mode, composed liveness. Its job is *coordinating a group of agents.* This is the capable one.
3. **The cross-boundary system** (in the external database) — members as a list with a **"shared" flag that is *closed by default*** (a channel stays internal unless explicitly published). Its job is *the boundary between your company and the outside* (this is what governs what an external client like ChatGPT could ever see).

They **deliberately mirror each other's shapes**, but they are **three separate systems with three separate stores.** This is the same split that showed up earlier as a tension (two things both called "channel" writing to different places).

**Why this matters for your dragnet idea (thinking for you):** when you say "a dragnet is attached to a channel and accessed by its members," there's a real question hiding in it — *which* channel system owns that relationship? The attachment machinery (which already holds the dragnet receipts) keys to a channel *by name* — so it can point at any of the three, but nothing yet says which is authoritative for "members who can read this dragnet." The capable member-registry (system 2) is the natural home, but the dragnet receipts currently attach through a path closer to system 1. **This is a seam to resolve, not a thing that's wrong** — but it's exactly the kind of "is this one thing or three things" question you'd want settled before building member-gated access, because the answer decides where access-rules live.
