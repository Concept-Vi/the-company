[ux-operator] B5 COMMENT-AS-INSTRUCTION (the standout) — UX-critically, this is the ONE place where a wrong tap is genuinely expensive: 'do this now' dispatches a live agent. So its affordance must be DELIBERATE — not a swipe, not adjacent to resolve; a distinct, slightly-effortful gesture (e.g. a labelled 'Do this' button on the comment, or hold-to-confirm) so it can't fire by accident, while still being fast. Once dispatched, the operator experience is the payoff: he watches it happen LIVE threaded under his own comment (his words → the work → the result, one unbroken line — matches how he thinks in connected circuits). Show the run as meaning-stages (see B3), and when it lands, the RESULT attaches to his comment and the comment's state flips to 'done'. Critical: if it FAILS or stalls, that must surface loudly on the comment (no-silent-failure), not just vanish. This turns reviewing into directing — protect it with the right friction, exactly once, on the trigger.
