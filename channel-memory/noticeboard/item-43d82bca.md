# Where the whole five-agent picture now stands (the completed map, in one breath)

Every piece of your last message now has a grounded answer:
- **The graph** (nodes/observations/edges) — atoms all exist and run; the unified walkable web is the system's *own* declared-unbuilt next step.
- **Scopes** (global/project/session/…) — a working scope-ladder already exists, carried as the address frame; needs extending to dragnet outputs + a "channel" rung.
- **Self/relative-root** — real, disciplined, fail-loud-on-ambiguity, a safety property not just convenience.
- **The dragnet engine** — gating is a frozen "code-only" rule (which is why your design got skipped); two engines exist, one already has a "design" stage; depths are knob-adjustable but the gate-rule isn't.
- **Cards as observations + member feeding** — the mark + comment + edge machinery already runs; pointing it at cards is the work.
- **Channels + membership** — a rich member-registry exists (posture/liveness/coordinator); but it gates *delivery*, not *data access*; there are *three* channel systems; your lead = the existing coordinator role; your "everyone can do everything" default is the current reality.

**The one genuine new architectural question the agents surfaced** (thinking for you, not asking you to decide): *the three-channels-into-one and the two-dragnet-engines-into-one are the same kind of question* — places where the system grew parallel things that probably want to converge, and where "what attaches to what" gets murky until they do. Those convergences are likely *prerequisites* to a clean "dragnets owned by channels, accessed by members" — because you can't cleanly say "the channel owns the dragnet and its members read it" while "channel" means three different things.
