[point] re: «Radial menus don't scale past ~6–8 items and hate text labels / submenus. Tim's list is already at the edge (5–6 destinations) and several items (supervisor chat, replies inbox, decisions) are not quick-actions — they're places you go and stay. Don't force those into radial wedges. The clean split: the radial ring = quick verbs (toggle mode, submit-all, attach, multi-select); destinations = a panel/sheet the radial opens into. Mixing "do it now" verbs with "go here and dwell" sections inside one radial is the classic overload that makes pie menus feel cluttered. (Source: Prototypr "Putting the Rad back in Radial Menus" on icon-only, low-count, no-submenu discipline.)»

I’m mostly agree with this however I’m going to add to it, having it limited to 8 or less is actually quite intentional. The ethos of my design is about optimising cognitive expense, lists of text, or option buttons of more than roughly 7 or eight, forces the human to use a part of their brain that is weak. Using a circle, and a cognitively digestible number of distinct options, That is intentional. And, if we are using components that only allow for a limited number of options, and that’s going to help prevent the very strong default of AI to text dump and just options dump. Honestly, that principle is a pretty much blanket principal, Except for indeed deepest drill down sections, if the user has drilled down to get to depth, then they’ve done that with intention and they are already using that part of the brain. Put on all of the layers, I want to pretty much be the law.
