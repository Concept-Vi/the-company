[point] re: «Accessibility & voice lens — one-handed use, a voice-first path (you prefer voice)»

Yes to this, I also prefer minimal text. That doesn’t mean minimal information, but reading words is one of the most cognitively expensive things, and makes it very time-consuming. The more I have to read and the more I have to think in order to understand it, And the more I have to Describe and input to give responses back to things. As a communication design, minimising text through digital spatial designs, interactive components, visual components, diagrammatic things, things I can tap on to get more information, yes you are right but I want voice and doing it with one hand is one of my preferred ways of using it, that was good insight, this is some extra information for you.
