## 7. The one thing I'd warn against

A tree-only, node-only selection model is *cleaner* than how reviewers actually think, and the gap will bite. We mark **spans, points, and ranges** as fluidly as nodes — an insert-point *between* two words, a range *across* three paragraphs, a single character. The block-hierarchy is excellent for "comment on this paragraph / this section," but if it's the *only* primitive, Tim will repeatedly want to mark something the tree can't name and feel the tool fighting him. Keep the elegant subtree gesture as the **default**, but make sure the substrate (the envelope's anchor) can express a *character-range across nodes*, a *point*, and a *node* — three anchor kinds, one envelope. Get that into the schema early (BD-K calls the envelope "the lynchpin") because retrofitting anchor-flexibility later is the kind of thing that forces a rewrite.
