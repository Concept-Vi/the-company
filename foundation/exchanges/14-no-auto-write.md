# 14 — Don't auto-write to the foundation

> [!info] Primary source — verbatim
> Reproduced from session `68e14d07` (2026-05-27). Issued immediately after I auto-captured the compositional-architecture exchange into the foundation. The contents written that turn were left in place at Tim's instruction; the act itself is what the rule corrects, going forward.

## Tim's message (verbatim)

> just a note , At this stage I don't like it when you automatically write into those files. It doesn't give me a chance make any corrections or additions or to guide you in what to put in there . Leave what you did for now, but I want that in the future OK?

## My response (verbatim)

Understood, and noted as a standing change — no defensiveness, you're right.

**What I'm taking from it:** committing things into the foundation (TIM.md, the exchanges archive, the Company memory entries) is an act that should go *through you* — proposed, open to your corrections/additions/guidance — not something I do unilaterally at the end of a message. The capture itself has value (you affirmed that earlier, and it's why the archive exists), but **what lands in the canonical record, and how it's framed, is yours to steer.** I was treating "the conversation is the construction" as license to auto-commit; the refinement is: *construct with you, write on your nod.*

**New default going forward:** after a substantive exchange, I'll **propose** the capture — show you what I'd write or describe it — and you guide / correct / add / approve before it goes in. You can also just say "write it" and I will. The detail-gathering and drafting still happen (so the value isn't lost); the difference is the gate.

I've left what I wrote this turn in place, per your instruction.

One genuine question on the future flow, so I match how you want it: when we get to a capture point, would you rather I **(a)** give you a short "here's what I'd record" summary to react to, **(b)** show you the actual drafted text before saving, or **(c)** something looser — you tell me in the moment? My lean is (a) for speed with (b) on request, but it's your call — and this preference is exactly the kind of thing I'd normally save so I don't make you repeat it, which I won't do until you tell me how you want it recorded.

## What this produced

- The **Guided commitment** principle in `../system/principles.md`.
- A standing rule for all agents: dense Tim-messages are not silent triggers for foundation writes. Tim controls what becomes canonical.

## Open at this exchange

- The exact form of the proposal ("here's what I'd record" summary vs. drafted text vs. ad-hoc) is not fixed; Tim sets it in the moment.
