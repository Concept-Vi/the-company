## §2 — THE LAWS (each of Tim's lines → a law, its why, and how it is verified)

### L1 — The Minimum Benchmark is FULL VISUAL INVESTIGATION + USE, not "it renders"
"Have you done a full visual investigation and use through the browser? That's the minimum benchmark... it's
not just looking to see if things render, or seeing if things work, it's how it looks and feels altogether."
- **Law:** "Done" is never code-reading, never "no error," never "the element exists." Done is: *driven through
  the browser as a whole experience* — every surface, every state, every transition, at every viewport and
  orientation — and judged on **how it looks and feels altogether**.
- **Why:** Tim recognises by sight and feel, not by reading. A feature that works but feels wrong is not done.
- **Verify:** every criterion is checked by USE (drive it), by a SEPARATE design-critic on whole-screen
  captures, at desktop + mobile-portrait + mobile-landscape. The gestalt is the unit of judgment, not the widget.

### L2 — TEXT-MINIMAL: text is cognitive load; minimize it ruthlessly
"The more text on screen the more my brain has to compute and the worst it will be."
- **Law:** Text is the most expensive thing on screen. The default is **no text**. Meaning is carried by
  position, shape, colour, motion, and size first; text appears only where nothing else can carry it, and only
  when wanted (see L6). A text-wall is a failure even if every word is correct.
- **Why:** Tim's perception is the compute ("his brain is the algorithm") — render so perception does the work,
  not reading.
- **Verify:** measure on-screen text density per surface; a default view should read at a glance with words as
  accents, not paragraphs. The readout cards become *visual* (a shape, a bar, a glyph) with words on demand.
  **[EXTENSION]** Adopt a hard budget: a resting surface shows only the few words that *are* the data (a name,
  a count), everything else is geometry; detail-text is revealed by intent (hover/tap/dwell), never resident.

### L3 — SMOOTHNESS: glitchy / jagged / out-of-proportion / not-smooth = waste; NO TELEPORTING
"The more glitchy or jagged out of proportion or otherwise not smooth the more waste... effects and animations
for everything, no teleporting."
- **Law:** Every change of state is *animated* — nothing appears, disappears, moves, or resizes by teleport.
  Elements keep identity and transition (shared-element motion, eased). Nothing is jagged, mis-proportioned, or
  abrupt. Motion is the connective tissue that makes the whole feel alive and continuous.
- **Why:** Discontinuity costs cognition and breaks the feeling of one living instrument.
- **Verify:** no instant pops; panels/menus/points/values all tween; layout is proportionate at every size
  (a measured grid/scale, never ad-hoc); 60fps-smooth, no jank. A "teleport audit": every appear/disappear/
  move on every interaction must have a transition. **[EXTENSION]** A single motion system (shared easing
  curves, durations, spring) governs everything so motion feels authored, not per-component improvised.

### L4 — THE FRONT END IS HALF THE BUILD
"This is as important as the entire backend, it is half of the entire build."
- **Law:** Form is not polish at the end. It is half of "done," held to the same evidence bar as the backend,
  and funded with the same depth (loop-prep, workflows, critics, acceptance-by-use).
- **Verify:** the surface gets its own completion-criteria truth table, its own implementation guide, its own
  research synthesis — the full three-document loop, not an afterthought.

### L5 — NATIVE on desktop AND mobile; mobile has EQUALLY REFINED portrait AND landscape, for EVERYTHING
"It needs to be native and intuitive on both desktop and mobile, on mobile it must have native and equally
refined landscape and portrait modes for everything."
- **Law:** Three first-class form factors — **desktop, mobile-portrait, mobile-landscape** — each *designed*,
  not "responsive-degraded." Every surface, control, panel, and gesture is refined in all three. Mobile is not
  a shrunk desktop; landscape is not a rotated portrait — each is native to its frame.
- **Why:** Tim uses this on his phone (Tailscale + PWA, working) and on desktop; both are primary.
- **Verify:** drive EVERY surface at 1440×900, 390×844 (portrait), and 844×390 (landscape). Each must feel
  native and complete — no clipped panels, no unreachable controls, no orientation that's an afterthought.
  **[EXTENSION]** Define explicit layouts per form factor (e.g. desktop = the instrument centered with
  ambient side strata; portrait = the instrument with a bottom-sheet stratum and a thumb-reachable control
  arc; landscape = the instrument with a side rail) — authored, not emergent.

### L6 — PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE: information there when wanted, gone when not
"The look and feel as a theme needs to be text minimal, intuitive, information there when I wanted and not when
I don't."
- **Law:** The resting state is calm and near-empty. Detail is summoned by intent and recedes when done. The
  surface breathes — it offers the next thing without demanding attention. Nothing is permanently on screen
  that isn't permanently needed.
- **Why:** "information there when I want it and not when I don't" — the patterned-visibility loop; the surface
  reveals on demand, hides by default.
- **Verify:** a cold-open surface is legible in one glance with almost no text; every deeper layer is one
  intent away and dismisses cleanly (animated, L3). No modal pile-ups, no persistent chrome that isn't earning
  its place. **[EXTENSION]** A consistent disclosure grammar: peek (hover/dwell) → open (tap/click) → pin
  (deliberate) → dismiss (anywhere/gesture), the same everywhere so it's learned once.

### L7 — THE VISUAL LANGUAGE: paper, not sci-fi; colours, polygons, soft shading, elegant
"Colours, polygons, subtle and soft shading, effects and animations for everything... Treat this as a totally
fresh design, feeling more like paper than sci-fi, subtle and aesthetic and elegant."
- **Law:** A **fresh** aesthetic — warm, tactile, **paper-like**: soft light grounds, gentle elevation and
  shadow (not glows), restrained colour used meaningfully (colour carries data, never decoration-for-its-own-
  sake), **polygons** as the structural motif (the seed's geometry is the visual language — see §3), subtle
  soft shading, refined typography. Elegant and quiet. The opposite of neon-on-black sci-fi.
- **Why:** Tim wants calm, aesthetic, elegant — a surface that feels like a beautifully made instrument or a
  fine sheet of paper, not a dashboard.
- **Verify:** the palette, elevation, type scale, and geometry are a coherent *designed* system (a fresh token
  set — see §6), applied everywhere; nothing reads as the old dark harness. A designer would call it elegant.
  **[EXTENSION]** Paper-isms: soft off-white/warm-neutral grounds; ink-like text used sparingly; shadow as
  *paper lift* (diffuse, low) not as drop-box; colour as *pigment* (a few harmonious hues with intent);
  polygons (the seed's box/circle/grid/sectors) as the literal scaffolding, rendered with soft fills + hairline
  edges. Light mode is the home; an optional dark "dusk paper" can come later, but paper-light leads.

### L8 — SEAMLESS, DECEPTIVELY SIMPLE, FULL POWER
"Panels and menu and actions that feels seamless and are deceptively simple but with no less capability or
control."
- **Law:** The surface looks effortless and uncluttered, yet exposes the FULL capability and control of the
  system underneath. Simplicity is achieved by *disclosure and motion* (L3, L6), never by removing power.
  Panels and menus flow into one another seamlessly; actions feel like one gesture.
- **Verify:** for every capability the backend offers (drive a binding, a centre, a pole, a dial, a rung, a
  registry, the scrubber, selection→builder, etc.), there is a seamless, simple way to reach it — and a power
  user loses nothing. No capability is dropped for the sake of looking clean.

### L9 — A COMPLETE INTERACTIVE APPLICATION — live, multipurpose; NOT a viewer
"This is not a viewer, this is a complete interactive application, live and multipurpose."
- **Law:** The surface is an *application*, not a read-only display. It is **live** (real-time, the present
  moves, data streams in), **interactive** (everything can be driven), and **multipurpose** (it serves the
  many strata of the Company, not one chart). It is the place Tim *operates from*.
- **Verify:** real-time updates (SSE) with no reload; every element is drivable; the surface hosts the multiple
  purposes already built this session (the instrument's lenses, plus the operable strata) under one coherent
  shell. **[EXTENSION]** "Multipurpose" = one shell, many *surfaces/strata* that share the design language,
  the motion system, the address+context spine, and flow into each other without mode-switch jarring.

### L10 — THE ONE THING TO KEEP: ADDRESSES ON THE UI + CONTEXT WRITTEN IN AND ATTACHED TO THEM
"You need to follow the established pattern of addresses on UI and context written in them and attached to the
address, but you should not follow any of the rest of the UI, don't even look at it."
- **Law:** PRESERVE and build on the **address-on-UI substrate**: every UI element carries a `ui://` address;
  context is written into the address and attached to it (the relational spine — Principal → Domain → Intent →
  … flows through addressed nodes). This is the ONE inheritance. Everything else in the current UI is the
  **disposable harness** — do not look at it, do not follow it, treat the design as totally fresh.
- **Why:** the address+context pattern is the relational backbone that makes the surface part of the Company's
  one circuit (addressing, context resolution, the RHM). It is load-bearing architecture, not styling.
- **Verify:** every element in the new surface is addressed (`ui://…`) with its context attached, resolvable
  the same way; the rest is built clean from the design language in §6, owing nothing to the old harness.
  **[ACTION]** Research wave 1 must locate and document the exact address-on-UI + context-attach mechanism
  (where `ui://` addresses are minted, how context is written into/attached to them) so the fresh surface
  inherits *only* that, faithfully.

### L11 — METHOD: loop-prep + dynamic workflows, integrating the equation and everything this session
"You would use loop prep and dynamic workflows to get the highest quality both in my experience and in the
architecture and in the integration of my equation and the systems and everything else that has been done and
covered over this session. No ceiling."
- **Law:** Build via the three-document loop-prep methodology (Completion Criteria / Implementation Guide /
  Research Synthesis), driven by dynamic multi-agent workflows (explore → design → implement → critique →
  refine), at the highest quality with no ceiling. Quality is measured on four axes at once: **Tim's
  experience**, the **architecture**, the **integration of his equation**, and the **integration of the
  systems/everything built this session**.
- **Constraint (autonomous-spawn-lead-only):** THIS session is the only autonomous builder; spawned agents do
  the exploring/designing/critiquing/implementing but must NOT arm their own autonomous self-modifying loops.

## §6 — THE FRESH DESIGN LANGUAGE (to be specified in the loop-prep docs) [EXTENSION — open, Tim corrects]

A fresh token system, NOT the old dark harness. First articulation (to be refined in research + design waves):
- **Ground:** warm off-white / soft paper neutrals; gentle, low-contrast surfaces; elevation by *diffuse soft
  shadow* (paper lift), not borders or glows.
- **Ink:** restrained, near-black warm ink for the few words; never pure black, never neon.
- **Pigment:** a small harmonious palette where colour MEANS (sector hue = angle, the one geometry-colour
  inheritance worth keeping; a few accent pigments for state — born/forming, fit/pile, lean A/B — soft, not
  saturated).
- **Geometry:** polygons (square, circle, grid, sectors, basins) as soft-filled shapes with hairline edges;
  the seed's proportions as the layout grid.
- **Type:** one refined typeface, a small modular scale; words are accents.
- **Motion:** one motion system — shared easing (e.g. eased-out cubic / gentle spring), consistent durations,
  shared-element transitions; nothing teleports; the present *breathes*.
- **Disclosure grammar:** peek → open → pin → dismiss, consistent everywhere.
- This becomes a real token file + primitives, replacing the harness palette wholesale.
