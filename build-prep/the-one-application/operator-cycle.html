The operator cycle

A decision is a channel-attached sequence of cards (present · explain · choose). The channel becomes your stack. The fabric works until it's blocked on you, you clear the stack, it resumes — and the cycle repeats. This is everything to build that, perfectly — function and form.

The bar on every single criterion — two-faced

Nothing is "done" on function alone. Both faces, verified by use and by sight, both viewports.

Function — by use

Real behaviour moves real state. No stub, no fake result. Drive it live and watch it work.

Form — by sight

Built from the design system via the resolver (no bespoke values, no hand breakpoints); a navigable visual surface, not text; at-bar by the reference + your 5.

The criteria — six groups

A · foundation

The decision-sequence type

  • a decision = an ordered sequence of ≥1 cards
  • card kinds: present · explain · choose
  • attached to a channel (data, not code)
  • item-type is a registry (add a kind = a row)

B · the queue

The channel-stack

  • the fabric pushes operator-items as it works
  • the stack is the surface's spine ("what needs you")
  • per-item clear-state (pending → cleared)
  • generalizes the "N waiting" inbox

C · the render

Every card at-bar

  • the keystone card (merge-sa) at-bar in flight
  • computed across portrait/landscape/desktop
  • host + card from one resolver (the fractal)
  • multi-card sequence + the RHM explanation

D · clearing

How you work the stack

  • decide-in-surface (the take = the write-back)
  • verify-in-surface (by sight + use) → unblocks
  • attribution floor — no agent can forge a clear
  • batch clearing in one sitting

E · the engine

The cycle itself

  • needs-you work becomes a stacked item (never a silent stall)
  • the blocked detector fires
  • resume on your clear → downstream flows
  • ≥2 full cycles, end-to-end, by use

F · integrity

Held across everything

  • all generated through the system (no stubs)
  • the design system is the source (one-change-propagates)
  • no ghost takes · no silent failure
  • the loop can map + judge itself

Order of attack (foundations first)

1 · the keystone card at-bar (merge-sa) — proof #1, in flight
2 · the decision-sequence type + card-kinds + item registry
3 · channel-attachment + the stack as the surface spine
4 · the multi-card sequence + the RHM explanation leg
5 · decide-in-surface + the attribution floor + batch clearing
6 · the cycle engine (produce → block → resume → repeat)
7 · the device-axis + whole-screen fractal (the resolver proof)
8 · verify-in-surface + per-item clear-state
9 · product-face + integrity (held throughout)

Done = two full operator-cycles run end-to-end on the live surface, by use AND by sight, both viewports: the fabric works → stacks a multi-card decision + a verify-request on a channel → goes blocked with a legible at-bar stack → you clear (decide + verify, attributed) → it resumes → stacks again → repeat. Nothing reaches you until at-bar by our own comparison.
