## 1. file identity (ID1) — KIND: identity
**meaning:** "When the company saves something, is the saved file 'the same thing' as the content written in it — or its own separate thing that points at the content? It matters when two saved things hold identical content: are they one thing, or two things that happen to match? And when content changes: is it a new thing, or the same thing changed?"
**options:**
- "Identity is the CONTENT — identical content is ONE thing" — implication: "Two files with the same content are recognised as one (no duplication); changing content makes a new identity. Clean dedup; but a file's own history/name is secondary to what it currently holds." [recommended-lean: the lead modelled BOTH-with-derivation; this is the content-half]
- "Identity is the FILE — each saved thing is its own thing" — implication: "Each file is itself, keeps its own name/history/place even if content matches another; content is what it holds, not what it IS. Preserves per-file provenance; but identical content lives in two places."
- "Both — a file is its own thing that RESOLVES TO shared content" — implication: "A file has its own identity AND points at content that can be shared/deduped underneath. Most expressive (the lead's BOTH-with-derivation); the residual to settle = is the dedup/cache one identity or two kinds of reference?"

## 2. channel member (ID2, residual) — KIND: identity
**meaning:** "When a work session ends, who stays as the 'member' in the company's channels — the PERSON behind it, or the SESSION itself? (Each session is a temporary working instance; the person persists across many.)"
**options:**
- "The PERSON is the durable member" — implication: "Channels remember the person across all their sessions; a session is just their current presence. Continuity of identity; sessions come and go under one member."
- "The SESSION is the member" — implication: "Each session is its own channel member with its own history; when it ends, that member closes. Precise per-session record; but no single persistent 'you' across sessions."
- "Both faces, reconciled" — implication: "A session is the live face, the person is the durable face; they reconcile. (The lead is reconciling the dual-face — this reaches Tim only if a residual survives that reconcile.)" [note: ID2 is a RESIDUAL — may not need Tim unless the reconcile leaves an open choice]

## 3. cluster (ID3) — KIND: identity
**meaning:** "A 'cluster' is a group of related things the company finds. Is a cluster something you NAME once and return to (a fixed, saved grouping) — or a LIVE grouping that recomputes as things change — or both?"
**options:**
- "A NAMED, saved thing" — implication: "You name a cluster and it stays put — a stable handle you return to. Predictable; but it goes stale as the underlying things change."
- "A LIVE, recomputing result" — implication: "A cluster is always the current grouping, recomputed from the latest — never stale; but no fixed handle to 'the cluster you named last week.'"
- "Both — a named handle over a live result" — implication: "You can name a cluster AND it recomputes underneath; the name is stable, the contents stay current. Most expressive; needs the rule for when the named handle and the live result diverge."

## 4. event streams (TH2, Shape-B) — KIND: addressability/shape
**meaning:** "The company's highest-volume live data — the running history log, the marks/notes/pins people leave — should each of those become a first-class thing you can point AT directly (address any single one), stay as references only (pointed at indirectly), or a mix?"
**options:**
- "First-class — point at any single one" — implication: "Every event/mark/note is directly addressable — you can link, recall, or decide on any one. Most powerful for memory/recall; but the highest-volume data becomes a huge address-space."
- "References only" — implication: "The streams stay bulk data, pointed at indirectly (by time/source), not each individually addressed. Lighter; but you can't directly reference a single moment."
- "A mix — the significant ones first-class, the bulk by reference" — implication: "Decisions/marks/notes become first-class; the raw high-volume log stays reference-only. Balances power and weight; needs the rule for which stream is which."

## 5. the form taxonomy (COV2) — KIND: taxonomy ★ SPECIAL SHAPE
**meaning:** "What are the REAL kinds of things in the company's content? Right now the kinds (decision / log / registry / prose) are a prior AI's guess, used to route how deeply each thing gets processed. Should the company keep that guess, or look at EVERYTHING first (an open, full-coverage pass) and let the real kinds emerge — then you author the true taxonomy from what's actually there?"
**options:**
- "Discover the kinds from coverage, then you author them" — implication: "Run the open full-coverage pass (describe what's actually there, no forced labels) → the natural kinds emerge → proposed to you → you author the real taxonomy. Grounded in reality; deeper-processing routing stays paused until it's recorded." [recommended — Tim's coverage law: the kinds come FROM the outputs]
- "Keep the current guess for now" — implication: "Use decision/log/registry/prose as-is to keep routing working — but it's scaffolding, likely wrong, and routes processing on a guess."
- "A mix — keep the guess running, refine from coverage as it arrives" — implication: "Don't block on the full pass; let coverage refine the kinds incrementally. Keeps momentum; the taxonomy is provisional longer."
**note:** COV2 isn't a clean pick — it's "approve discovering the real taxonomy from coverage." Its options are really about SEQUENCING the discovery vs the placeholder. Flagging the shape for the lead — this kind ("taxonomy") = evidence → proposed kinds → Tim authors, more than a 3-option pick.
