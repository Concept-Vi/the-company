*Tim-confirmed 2026-06-18. Provisional + growing (open-future, not closure). The shape + the load-bearing decision are settled; the wiring specifics are open and named below. Grounded in Tim's own prior corrections (recalled from corpus space='history' — cited).*

## The problem (Tim, verbatim-in-meaning, 2026-06-18)
"Working on partial knowledge + standard agent workflows will FAIL with certainty. Any SUBJECTIVE determination will fail. Having OBJECTIVE COMPLETENESS covered is a REQUIREMENT — but we haven't quite worked out how. To get the coverage and full scope, using the small models concurrently is the only way. But the reasoning / decisions / identification / judgement can't be done by the small models — only bigger models. The bigger models can't read all of the coverage, so they work TOGETHER: the small models make determinations and outputs in their lane; the big model judges. Graphs, levels of detail, structured outputs — I know there is a way but I don't know exactly."

## The mechanism (one organism, not steps)
- **MAP — coverage (small/local models, concurrent).** A swarm of small models, each on a CONTAINED unit (artefact + ONE unit), doing narrow DETERMINISTIC extraction/classification → structured outputs. Disjoint = free-breadth = objective coverage. **No judgment.** The signal is the GROUPING / DISTRIBUTION of outputs across all concurrent jobs — a pattern in the distribution triggers the next action — never any single model's verdict. A small model may determine only within a bound where the relevant registry slice fits its context.
- **COMPOSE — the substrate graph.** The structured outputs accrue INTO the company's own substrate (see THE DECISION) — a live, central, queryable graph of connectedness, navigable at chosen LEVELS OF DETAIL.
- **JUDGE — reduce (big model: cloud judge).** The big model navigates the GRAPH (never the raw mass it can't hold), reasons, identifies, judges, and re-dispatches the swarm where coverage is thin (the conditional cascade). It assesses COMPLETENESS as a whole-property (see THE KEY INSIGHT).

## ★ THE DECISION (Tim-confirmed 2026-06-18): the coverage graph IS the company's own substrate — NOT a parallel findings-map.
The graph the big model judges on is the company's substrate (every addressed thing + its typed relations); the small models' extractions become FIELDS / EDGES on existing addresses; "levels of detail" = the type hierarchy + the resolution axes. We do NOT build a separate findings-store laid beside the substrate.
- WHY: unions-not-bridges. One connectedness registry; every consumer (and the big-model judge) resolves through the one substrate. A parallel findings-map would be an island.
- This is the SAME instinct Tim applied to #69 self-id minutes earlier ("don't build a parallel marker — reuse the one registration"). Confirmed twice on two problems.
- It is what "the substrate under the graph migrates INTO the company" (DNA, 2026-06-18) already implied. The graph DNA renders IS the coverage graph.

## ★ THE KEY INSIGHT — completeness is a whole-property, and it's the same engine as the union method
Objective completeness is NOT "the small models read everything." A single unit cannot know what the whole system needs (Tim, 2026-05-27: "asking individual small-model runs about system-level missing items is ineffective — a single file cannot know what the whole system needs"). So completeness is a property of the COMPOSED GRAPH measured against the INTENTION — the gap between (what the graph shows) and (what the intention requires) IS the deliverable map, judged at the reduce step, never per-unit. That is the **same as-is-vs-intention gap** Tim named for the union methodology. ⟹ **the coverage mechanism and the union methodology are the same engine pointed at different content:** small models map the as-is → the substrate holds it whole → the big model judges the gap to intention → the build extends the union to fill it.
