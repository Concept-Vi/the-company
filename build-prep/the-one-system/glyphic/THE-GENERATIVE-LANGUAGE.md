## 1 · The physics (the laws that hold everywhere, in every medium, at every scale)

**The root:** nothing has inherent identity; a thing is its relations. `Context + Perspective = Meaning`.
**The FRAME law** `[Tim, 2026-07-02]`: node ≡ frame ≡ type ≡ registry ≡ coordinate. Everything that views
(reader, query, sentence, glyphgraph, zone, an AI) is itself a node in the space it views; a view is a frame;
a frame is rules-of-projection over invariant data; ultimately ONE, relative to variable unity, invariant.

The laws, each `[Tim]` or `[ratified]` in the lineage, unified:

1. **Derive, never place.** Every value resolved, none typed in. Addresses are DERIVED from counts, never
   assigned. Hand-copies always fork; addresses can't. (The conservation law — a placed thing has opted out
   of the physics and rots.)
2. **Meaning is difference.** A value's significance is its delta from context; influence scales with
   difference. One law under both ends: near-imperceptible zones (below attention, so the eye rests) AND
   gold-as-rare-punctuation (large delta, so it lands). Applies along the temporal — everything does.
3. **Ration, differ, resolve locally.** The identity is defined by what it RATIONS (gold · octagon ·
   embossing · motion · the one focal · the star figures — one law, many dimensions: facet-rarity mirrors
   gold-rarity; signal = area × chroma and the budget balances itself). Meaning-as-difference governs
   everything continuous; nearest-scope-wins resolves everything contextual.
4. **Every boundary is the root of its own cascade** — of values (dials cascade down), of reading (rows
   chain under any root), of time (the ring law). **Two perpendicular motions at every boundary:** VALUES
   cascade DOWN; STRUCTURE composes UP (atom→molecule→organism→zone→screen). "Conflating them is how design
   systems rot." Selection = choosing a scope; then exactly two ops: re-dial or re-fill. **Sovereignty
   inside, treaty outside** (invariants are global; the cascade needs its police).
5. **Any part is a whole.** Cut-complete (skimming is reading at depth one — "descending never repairs a
   broken summary; it adds resolution to a complete one") + fragment-self-contained (any part travels) +
   **chain-additive: relations ADD, they never CARRY** (drop the "And…", the connector, the motion — sense
   survives; the motion end-frame rule is its special case). Together: any (node, depth) selection from the
   story-tree is a valid artifact.
6. **The reading is multiaxial and root-relative.** Two pure walks — DESCEND (deeper in one unit) and
   TRAVERSE (across a rank; the row itself tells the story; channels are rows: the figures alone, the focal
   elements alone, each must chain) — plus FIELDS (warmth/ordinal/density gradients you orient by, not
   read) and ARROWS (drawn verbs). **Any forward walk respecting the orderings is a valid reading** —
   diagonal hops legal BY LAW (fragment-self-containment = land anywhere; cut-completeness = change zoom
   mid-journey; chain-additivity = leaving a row costs momentum, never sense). **Reading a deck and
   navigating an app are the same object — a walk through one lattice.** Rows are WRITTEN, not assembled:
   descend by rows, refine by cells.
7. **Measure: children partition the parent's [0,1].** Units are SPANS, not points (slide 2 of 3 = [1/3,2/3);
   the point 2/3 is a SEAM). Nested fractions collapse a path to one decodable number (zone 3/5 in slide 2/3
   = 7/15). The story is a CONSERVED quantity; count decides slicing → fusion is conservation, not a trick
   (and the one-pager taught: tiers may FUSE or EXTRACT the strongest fragment). Counts cluster because
   ENCOUNTERS cluster — attractors at occasion-optima.
8. **The four spaces + the two halves.** SUBJECT (soft-edged sectors radial around an origin — the polygon) ·
   TELLING (a projection made by the two authored acts: the CUT and the ROUTE) · STRUCTURE (the sharp,
   calculable half) · READER (the demand side: held context, occasion ladder, goodness-as-FIT). The
   calculable half derives by rule; the authored half (what's worth saying, the cut, the route) is the
   resolver's INPUT. **The boundary between the halves MOVES with the reader** — jargon is an address
   (the expert carries the registry; the novice needs the value inlined; christening is once PER READER;
   expert density is address-density). Soft edges need judgment; sharp edges calculate — that difference IS
   the two-halves boundary, which is why the cut is the first authored act. **Purpose must be recorded** —
   without it, "good" is unjudgeable ("good is only relative to the purpose that thing was for").
9. **Motion is change; change is temporal; the temporal sits UNDER everything.** Three registers — MOVEMENT
   (the person travels) · TRANSITION (the content travels) · STATE (nothing travels; the same thing,
   differently) — a change must know its register. The laws (tours-direct evidence — shipped product):
   **the change budget** (bounded difference per hop; point density follows RATE-OF-CHANGE of the view, not
   distance) · anchors-and-changers (what stays orients, what changes informs) · **arrival is relative to
   the path** (a transition is a property of the EDGE; the deck's "And it's Difficult…" is verbal
   rotation-on-arrival; deep links land ORIENTED, anchors scaling with how cold the arrival is) · the
   anticipatory verb (descend=zoom, transport=slide, gather=convergence) · spatial persistence (off-screen
   is a place; nothing teleports; a reload is a broken seam) · shared-element continuity · **the ring law**
   (the boundary carries identity through change; interior change = state, boundary change = BECOMING —
   every-boundary-a-root applied in time; interior intensity is a spectrum: recolour < reflow < swap <
   sand) · the expert lane (skimming is the expert route; pave it) · **the transition IS the animated diff**
   (diff two views by address: unchanged = anchors, changed = changers, the anchor = the LCA of the changers'
   addresses — motion DERIVED, never authored) · **an artifact is a route between two states of
   understanding** (start = held context, destination = purpose; generation is NAVIGATION first;
   count ≈ understanding-distance ÷ change-budget) · **cut where the difference is** (the tours' point-
   placement = the subject-cut heuristic; seams belong at the content's fault lines; low-change sectors fuse).
   One law-set at five timescales: relationship · artifact · page · interaction · icon.
10. **The four machine kinds.** Every finding decomposes into TOKEN (value) · REGISTRY ENTRY (typed citizen)
    · RULE (derivation) · INVARIANT (checkable predicate). The recipe: name the vocabulary, enumerate the
    citizens, replace every choice with a derivation, make the law checkable. **Checkers are classed:**
    `predicate` (mechanical) · `build` (refuses to compile) · `model` (needs an AI judge) — the system knows
    which of its own laws require judgment.
11. **Provision, not only restraint.** The grammar must know what to PROVIDE, not just forbid: zones-not-
    plates (a zone is the ground breathed warmer, never a box; atmosphere BLEEDS, argument INSETS), diagram
    atmosphere (ambient+primary+detail — "a primary alone is a legend, not a world"), evidence density,
    nesting depth, controlled density, fill ("a void is not calm, it's absence"), iconography. The 80%
    lesson: the missing tier between molecules and layouts is ORGANISMS — "frames without furniture."
    Confident size, generous evidence, rationed gold.
12. **Emphasis is relational, never a rank.** No numeric priority anywhere ("priority can decrease
    compositionality" `[Tim]`) — one focal per frame, order = the written sequence, reflow = a policy,
    all scoped, nearest-scope-wins. Variants are compositions, not new shapes. Properties before new
    entries (gather(in|out), not a fourth verb).
13. **Identity resolves; instance is authored.** Identity values (colours, steps, sizes, radii) MUST be
    addresses; instance values (this mesh has seven nodes) are authorship that SNAPS to the scale.
    "Over-tokenize and you strangle the maker."
14. **Spacing is a VOCABULARY, not a grid** — a small set of named words (unit-counts on the 8-base; the
    near-prime climb 3/4/5/7/11), while type/radius climb the RATIO (×1.5). "A 13px gap is wrong because
    it's not a word in the language." Spacing/proportion = one relational system at two scales.
15. **Extract the rule, not the mistake.** The source holds the rules; it doesn't always obey them. Read
    intent through imperfection, regularize into tokens+rules. And: measure what's measurable as ANCHORS,
    then LOOK — "you can't work out rules objectively; you need to visually reason."
16. **Prediction-first.** Encode from principle BEFORE evidence arrives (Tim deliberately withholds), so
    every disagreement is signal. Evidence classes: source-direct · tours-direct · derived-from-principle
    (falsifiable). Never launder interpretation as source truth (primary-source-first; "secondhand lineage
    is not firsthand evidence").
17. **The instrument wears the identity it edits.** Every surface derives from the registries it serves;
    the app is the first artifact of its own language; its chrome obeys the voice (comb: one clause per
    lede, nothing said twice, instructions die where the control teaches itself). Never fix by subtraction
    (the studio lesson — the cure for "developer feel" is language + organization, not removing function).
    A pattern correct in one context is not a universal law (the footer-band lesson: deliberate-and-optional
    ≠ mandatory-global).
18. **Everything flows both ways.** Typed directional edges with equal-and-opposite, per type, level, scale
    `[Tim]`. Whatever can be shown can be pointed at; whatever can be generated can be read back. A one-way
    surface is a violation on par with a hardcoded hex.
