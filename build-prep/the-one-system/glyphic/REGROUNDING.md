## 3 · The concepts (durable ideas — the content, not scaffolding)
- **Glyphics is a visual LANGUAGE** — meaning composed from facets; **meaning is a FIELD, not a lookup**; facet=
  morpheme, glyphic=word/noun-phrase, edge=relation (visual, not a text label), container+slots=sentence; a
  **glyphgraph is a sentence drawn**; the read-out ("transglyphing") speaks it; test: "can you hear the octagon?"
- **glyphic = address** — glyphs, positions, types, registries are all coordinates in ONE address space; the design
  and Company address worlds are the same language at two altitudes.
- **The spatial theorem** — one operation `n/x` (divide a boundary, name the slot) does layout, form, depth,
  population; everything a ratio resolved against its parent (responsive+device-independent free); addresses are
  mixed-radix numbers (infinite, ordered, navigable); absence is addressable; fractal (a glyphic IS a graph at one
  zoom); level-of-detail/semantic-zoom; zones = named regions. Space is a facet of the language.
- **The AI fusion = UNION not bridge** — one AI registry, Company-home, CV_AI = browser projection; a model-cap
  BECOMES a role fired via run_role (no double-prompt); flip-to-Company = one registry op.
- **Context + Perspective = Meaning** (Tim's corpus formula) — the root; why meaning is contextual/field.
- **The Virtual Hub (ConceptV, Tim's 10-yr property-tech co.)** — the theory's PHYSICAL prototype: nodes at
  coordinates in a site-frame, computed placement, faces per stakeholder/stage, annotations bound to addresses.
  Proof the system already worked commercially before it had names. (The source corpus: universal-mechanics +
