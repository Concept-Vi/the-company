## Synthesis — the patterns

1. **There is really ONE schema.** Every used type converged on title/description/priority/issue_number + a per-type resolution/verification vocabulary. The hollow {"type":"object"} was almost honest: these types never differed by FIELDS — they differ by **lifecycle** and by **what proof closure requires**. Reconstruction = one shared core + per-type closure block, not seven bespoke schemas. (This is law 11 arriving from the data: types differ by their STATE declarations and closure requirements.)
2. **The state machines were declared but never wired.** task declares 4 states; every real post used open/resolved — the generic lifecycle silently overrode every per-type one. The declared status_values are the clearest never-completed intention in the set; **enforcement is the missing wire** (exactly what the rebuilt BOARD/REGISTRY organs supply).
3. **Usage flowed to wherever the concept was already real.** The three zero-post types are exactly the three whose concept already existed elsewhere. Hollow-and-unused ≠ abandoned — it means **redundant at birth**: FUSE, not RECONSTRUCT.
4. **The cascade was the point; the definitions were deferred.** Hollow inputs still produced complete fan-outs — the April wave exercised the generator; the meaning-backfill never came. **This report is that backfill.**
5. **The biggest glow is the evidence habit.** Unprompted, sessions attached resolved_by/verified_at/bugs_found/key_insight to closures — items carrying their own proof of done. Tim's evidence law emerging from lived usage; make it first-class in every reconstructed type.
