ConceptV Design System · Surface registry

A warm spatial interface,
spoken in tonal zones.

The system is differentiated through subtle undertones, not obvious colour blocks. Near-white tinted surfaces mark containment depth — each level nested inward adds ~2% undertone over the ground, so you read the structure without borders. Semantic tones (content, support, source, review) are an optional layer on top of that depth ladder. Gold is reserved for Vi, active decisions and selected states. Every wash is mixed from a named pigment toward the ground, scaled by one intensity knob: re-tune the whole language by moving a single token.

Zoning = containment depth made visible (warm near-white throughout — no cool greys). Use gold only for Vi, active decisions, selected states, and key emphasis. The differences should be felt more than seen.

The zone registry

The optional semantic tones — each a pigment mixed toward the ground at a low percentage (the tint shown is at the system's default intensity). The primary zoning is depth (see core/containers.css); these are the opt-in category layer on top.
