The Colour Axis

Tokens are the matter (the hex, written once in colors_and_type.css); the axis is the grammar. It adds what a token can't know about itself — its family, the legible ink it pairs on, its structural roles, whether it holds across theme flips, and whether it's an alias. Families are a small relational graph (voice vs structure, who-pairs-on-whom), computed once and read everywhere. Everything below is rendered live from window.CV_AXES.resolve('color') — a projection of the axis, not a copy.

Families — a relational graph

Each family declares what it's for, whether it's a saturated voice or quiet structure, and the foreground family it pairs on. A rule lives at the most general level it's true; values only override when they genuinely differ.

Values — self-describing

Every value points at its single-source token. The pairing column shows the value as a background with its legible ink drawn on it — resolved by CV_AXES.pairCSS(), the one home for "text on a colour" (it dissolved the hardcoded --on-gold). 🔒 = theme-invariant (holds on dark).

One axis · different families per type

A component type declares which families its colour slot resolves — the value-side twin of a socket's accepts. Same axis, different declared subscription. A glyphic mark can't pick a zone wash; a surface can't pick brand gold. The picker is just the axis filtered by the type's slot rule.
