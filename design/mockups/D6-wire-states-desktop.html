When a build doesn't cleanly close
The wire's internal pipeline is backstage — you never see claude -p mechanics. What reaches you is the return: six ways a build comes back, each as a card in your inbox at ui://inbox/build-review. Every one is loud and recoverable — nothing fails into silence.

The change was made, but verification (affected suites + drift + adversarial) didn't pass. It did not close implemented; the reason is attached. Send back with a why, or look at what failed.

Changed files exceeded the declared scope. An empty declared scope is deny-all, so an undeclared build can never silently close. The diff is here; approve the wider scope, or send it back tighter.
